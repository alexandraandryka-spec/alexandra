2026 | Berlin

ITB Berlin 2026. A journey around the world in the shadow of global uncertainty

This year's ITB Berlin was an exceptional professional experience for me. For years, this event has been one of the most important meeting points for the global travel industry, and in 2026 it carried an additional meaning as it celebrated its 60th anniversary.

At moments, ITB Berlin felt like a journey around the world contained within just a few exhibition halls. A little like an industry Expo, where in a single day you could move from Asia, through the Middle East, to Latin America, observing not only what each country offers, but also how each destination wants to tell its story to the world.

I had the opportunity to take part in this event thanks to CFI Hotels Group, which I represented together with Aleksandra from Premier Krakow Hotel. The two Aleksandras turned out to be a true Dream Team: one full of energy, the other a calm and steady presence. In practice, it meant a very good balance of personalities, pace and ways of working.

For us, participating in the fair was an opportunity to meet industry representatives from different parts of the world, represent the hotels and properties of CFI Hotels Group, build new business contacts and observe the trends shaping the future of tourism.

This year's edition, however, took place at a very particular moment. The conflict in the Middle East and concerns about its possible escalation were a clear backdrop to many conversations. In tourism, such events are never abstract. Security, public sentiment and the geopolitical situation very quickly influence travellers' decisions, the destinations they choose and the activity of entire markets.

This was also visible at the fair itself. Some impressive pavilions, including those of Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates, seemed prepared for a much larger number of visitors. Beautifully designed, polished and striking spaces showed the enormous potential of these destinations, while also reminding us how sensitive tourism remains to global tensions.

For me, taking part in ITB Berlin 2026 was not only a chance to represent CFI Hotels Group, but also an opportunity to look at the industry from a broader perspective. These were intense days of observation and conversation, showing that the future of tourism will depend not only on attractive offers, technology or trends, but also on trust, stability, a sense of safety and human relationships.

I returned from Berlin convinced that being present at events like this still matters greatly, even in the age of the internet and online communication. Because in tourism, more than in many other industries, relationships are built first and foremost between people.

2013 | South Africa

Memory from my stay in South Africa

My stay in South Africa in 2013 was one of those journeys I still remember vividly. It was a time of intense observations, encounters with extraordinary nature and a daily reality very different from the one I knew before.

One of the places that made a particularly strong impression on me was Kirstenbosch, one of the nine National Botanical Gardens managed by SANBI in South Africa.

Kirstenbosch is the oldest and largest botanical garden in South Africa and the third-largest botanical garden in the world. An article inspired by that visit was later published in the journal Wszechświat.
